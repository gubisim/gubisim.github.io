The First Date

It all started with Tinder in a little place called Logan Utah. Over the course of a year and a half we ran into each others profiles and each time there was something that drew us to each other and we matched. We talked a little bit and even exchanged phone numbers but for some reason we never could seem to get a date together.

In the fall of 2015 we matched a final time and were able to finally set up a date, which Cassidy canceled, once... and then twice (She was sick, give her a break already.) Brad thought his chances were not looking so good but felt like she genuinely wanted to go out. Desipte feeling like a desperate fool, Brad, set up a date with Cassidy for a 3rd time. On November 18th, 2015, it finally happened. We met up to go for Liege Waffles at The Waffle Iron (who would later cater our wedding reception). All the pains, aches, and awkwardness of a first date seemed to not exist. Their was an instant level of comfort, understanding of each others sense of humor, and connection formed that night.

The Proposal

We continued to date and fall in love over the next several months and on June 9th, 2016, Brad popped the big question. In Cassidy's words "he took me to dinner at the waffle iron, where we had our first date. After that he told me we were going to go to "the block" (a huge concrete block by the canyon in Logan that looks over all of cache valley) where we went on our third date. As we were walking on the little path in between tons of bushes that leads to the block we turned a corner and all of the sudden I saw a bunch of tiny candles flickering, lanterns all throughout the trees, and on top of the block I saw a couch. The couch that was in the apartment of where Brad lived last year when we started dating. This couch is very special, it's where we first held hands, cuddled, had our first kiss, where he first told me he loved me, and where we first talked about marrying each other. I love how many happy memories this couch has and constantly have told Brad how much I miss it since he moved to a new place for the summer. He went to his old apartment and asked the strangers who now live there if he could borrow it for this special night. I could not believe what I was seeing. I sat on the couch (freaking out and saying oh my gosh over and over) and Brad got down on one knee, told me the sweetest things I've ever heard, and asked me to marry him. I cannot believe that he asked ME to be his future wife!!!"
